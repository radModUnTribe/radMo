# RadMo — Spider Shape Personas

Five archetypes derived from recognizable spider chart shapes. Each persona describes an epistemic style, not a political position. The same shape can appear across the political spectrum.

See `product/features.md` Section 7 for the full dimension definitions that underpin these profiles.

---

## The Five Archetypes

### 1. The Bubble Scholar
*Factual Grounding: 85 | Cross-Viewpoint: 15 | Source Diversity: 20 | Claim Integrity: 80 | Overall: ~52*

**Shape:** Tall on the left (Factual Grounding + Claim Integrity), nearly flat on the right (Cross-Viewpoint + Source Diversity).

**Profile:** Rigorous within their world — every normative claim has factual scaffolding, sources are accurately represented. But the bubble is tight: all citations from the same ideological corner, never validated outside their tribe. Smart and careful, but untested under adversarial scrutiny.

**Tim Urban axis:** Lawyer-mode (rigorous variant). Builds careful arguments but only tests them against allies.

**Copy:** *"The rigor is real; the bubble is equally real."*

**Real-world examples:**
- **Paul Krugman** — meticulous within his framework, sources accurately cited, but almost exclusively validated by center-left economists. His spider would be tall on the left, nearly flat on the right.
- **Ben Shapiro** — same shape, opposite political direction. Legally rigorous in argument construction, rarely misrepresents direct sources. But citation universe is almost entirely conservative media, cross-viewpoint validation near zero.
- **Jon Stewart** — high claim integrity (when he cites something, it's real), strong factual grounding for a media figure. But cross-viewpoint validation is nearly zero — conservatives dismiss him as partisan entertainment. Bubble Scholar despite genuine rigor.

**Note on political symmetry:** Krugman and Shapiro have nearly identical spider shapes despite being on opposite ends of the political spectrum. This is the point — the credibility score is about *how* you think, not *what* you think.

---

### 2. The Vibes Merchant
*Factual Grounding: 10 | Cross-Viewpoint: 20 | Source Diversity: 15 | Claim Integrity: 25 | Overall: ~17*

**Shape:** Collapsed polygon near the center. Almost no shape at all.

**Profile:** Posts almost entirely in ought-space with no factual scaffolding. Unverified claims, no sourcing, no cross-viewpoint engagement. The shape itself communicates "low signal" at a glance — pure proclamation.

**Tim Urban axis:** Zealot-mode. The verdict is always the starting point; evidence is incidental.

**Real-world examples:**
- **Iran guy** — the platform's canonical example. BREAKING claims, insider sources, no corroboration, never corrects the record.
- **Anonymous breaking-news accounts** on X/Twitter — accounts with large followings that post BREAKING every few days, always have insider sources, never link to anything, wrong 80% of the time. Survive because being first matters more than being right.
- **RFK Jr. (2021–2023)** — normative claims arrive constantly, factual grounding thin or actively misrepresented, sources when cited don't support claims, cross-viewpoint validation near zero outside a specific conspiratorial slice.

---

### 3. The Magpie
*Factual Grounding: 30 | Cross-Viewpoint: 55 | Source Diversity: 85 | Claim Integrity: 40 | Overall: ~50*

**Shape:** Wide, lumpy spike on Source Diversity. Decent cross-viewpoint. Weak everywhere else.

**Profile:** Reads voraciously, cites from everywhere, gets engagement across the spectrum. But normative claims are loosely grounded and sources don't always say what they claim. Impressively broad information diet, deployed sloppily.

**Tim Urban axis:** Lawyer-mode (well-read variant). The breadth creates an impression of rigor that the claim integrity doesn't back up.

**Copy:** *"Cosmopolitan information diet, loosely deployed."*

**Real-world examples:**
- **Malcolm Gladwell** — reads voraciously across disciplines, cites a wide range of sources and geographies, gets engagement from very different audiences. But sources frequently don't support the sweeping conclusions he draws from them. Academic researchers regularly document the misreads.
- **Fareed Zakaria** — similar pattern. Cites widely, engages across political lines, clearly well-read globally. The connecting tissue between citations and conclusions is often shakier than it appears.

---

### 4. The Persuader
*Factual Grounding: 50 | Cross-Viewpoint: 80 | Source Diversity: 45 | Claim Integrity: 55 | Overall: ~60*

**Shape:** Single prominent spike on Cross-Viewpoint. Other dimensions decent but not impressive.

**Profile:** Validated across tribes — but probe the sources and it gets murky. Rhetorically skilled enough that people nod along without checking the receipts. High cross-viewpoint validation *looks* like the Radical Moderate shape but the underlying work isn't there.

**This is the most dangerous archetype for RadMo's matching system** — their overall score and CV spike mimic a Radical Moderate at a glance. The *shape* is the tell: a single spike rather than a full polygon.

**Tim Urban axis:** Lawyer-mode (charismatic variant). The persuasion is real; the rigor underneath it is inconsistent.

**Real-world examples:**
- **Barack Obama** — consistently validated across tribal lines in a way almost no other political figure achieves. But probe policy-adjacent claims and the sourcing is often curated for rhetorical effect rather than epistemic completeness.
- **Yuval Noah Harari** — *Sapiens* gets nodding agreement from across the political and intellectual spectrum. But historians and specialists in every domain he touches regularly document oversimplification, misrepresentation, or overstated conclusions.

---

### 5. The Radical Moderate
*Factual Grounding: 82 | Cross-Viewpoint: 78 | Source Diversity: 75 | Claim Integrity: 80 | Overall: ~79*

**Shape:** Full, nearly symmetrical polygon. No dramatic spikes, no obvious holes.

**Profile:** Not perfect on any single dimension, but no obvious weak spots. The shape reads as balanced at a glance — you don't need the numbers. This is the target archetype the platform is designed to produce more of.

**Tim Urban axis:** Scientist-mode. Evidence-first, willing to update, seeks disconfirmation.

**Real-world examples:**
- **Derek Thompson** — closest to Radical Moderate of any mainstream journalist. Leads with data, cites across disciplines and political lines, respected by researchers across the spectrum. Not flashy about it — the shape emerges from doing the epistemic work, not from branding as a centrist.
- **Tyler Cowen** — economist, libertarian-leaning, but consistently validated by people who disagree politically. Cites across format, geography, and ideology. Rarely misrepresents sources. Changes his mind publicly and explains why. Probably the closest to a genuine Radical Moderate in the pundit ecosystem.

## Spider Shape Evolution Over Time

Several real-world figures demonstrate that spider shapes are not fixed — they drift over time, typically in response to audience capture dynamics and financial incentives.

### Sam Harris
**Early (2010–2015):** Near-Radical Moderate. FG: 78, CV: 60, SD: 60, CI: 70. Overall: ~68. Validated across secular, liberal, and some conservative intellectual circles. Genuine source diversity.

**Late (2020–present):** Bubble Scholar. FG: 75, CV: 30, SD: 42, CI: 52. Overall: ~52. Grounding remains (the philosopher's habit). But audience narrowed to anti-woke cluster. Cross-viewpoint validation collapsed. Citation universe shrank. Claim integrity degraded on contested empirical topics.

**What drove the drift:** Audience capture. The financial and social incentives of a narrowed but intensely loyal audience outweighed the epistemic incentives of broader engagement.

### Joe Rogan
**Early JRE (2012–2018):** Persuader with wide CV. FG: 22, CV: 72, SD: 62, CI: 22. Overall: ~42. Genuinely wide guest range — scientists, comedians, fighters, journalists across the spectrum. Grounding and claim integrity were always weak; the curiosity was real.

**Late JRE (2020–present):** Persuader shell / Vibes Merchant core. FG: 25, CV: 65, SD: 32, CI: 18. Overall: ~34. Guest list narrowed toward contrarian cluster. Source diversity dropped. Claim integrity further degraded during COVID period. The warmth is real; the epistemic floor collapsed.

### Bret Weinstein
**Pre-Evergreen (pre-2017):** Legitimate Bubble Scholar. FG: 65, CV: 52, SD: 50, CI: 62. Overall: ~57. Solid evolutionary biology work. Decent cross-viewpoint engagement within academia.

**Post-Evergreen (2020–present):** Bubble Scholar gone rogue. FG: 48, CV: 18, SD: 22, CI: 32. Overall: ~32. Scientific framing applied to domains where factual base is thin. Citation universe collapsed into self-referential contrarian cluster. Claim integrity documented failures on ivermectin and vaccine data.

## The Asymmetric Rigor Failure Mode

A pattern that cuts across all personas: **applying rigorous evidential standards to outgroup claims while relaxing them for ingroup claims.** This is not a new type of bad actor — it's a failure mode that can affect any persona, and it's financially self-reinforcing.

**How it works:** You don't lose your rigor all at once. You preserve it where it earns applause (scrutinizing the outgroup) and relax it where it costs you (scrutinizing the ingroup). The financial signal from audience capture reinforces this asymmetry at every step.

**The signal:** A user whose Claim Integrity score is 85 for sources that agree with them but 30 for sources that disagree — the *gap* between ingroup and outgroup CI is the asymmetric rigor signal.

**Potential dimension:** Directional Claim Integrity — track CI separately for ingroup-confirming vs. ingroup-challenging sources. The gap between them is the asymmetric rigor score. This is buildable as a more granular version of Claim Integrity with a directional lens applied.

**Real-world examples:** Rogan (applies curiosity selectively), Weinstein (applies scientific rigor selectively), Stewart (applies precision selectively). The failure mode is not politically directional — it appears across the spectrum.

## Polarization Thesis — Expected Spider Drift at Population Level

If polarization is increasing, we'd expect these directional shifts across the user population:

- **Cross-Viewpoint Validation** — shrinking across the board; tribal hardening reduces unexpected validation; distribution shifts from bell curve toward bimodal (small cluster of high-CV Radical Moderates, large mass of low-CV accounts)
- **Source Diversity** — narrowing; algorithmic feeds and newsletter culture self-select toward ideological neighborhoods; geographic diversity collapses fastest
- **Factual Grounding** — ambiguous; polarization doesn't necessarily reduce rigor within bubbles; Bubble Scholars can become *more* rigorous as they become more siloed
- **Claim Integrity** — degrading; audience capture deepens the incentive to misrepresent adversarial sources; asymmetric rigor becomes the norm

**Net effect:** More Bubble Scholars and Vibes Merchants. Fewer genuine Radical Moderates. Magpie archetype may *increase* — algorithmic feeds drive broad consumption but shallow processing.
