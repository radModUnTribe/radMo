# Trend 05: Institutional Trust Is at Historic Lows

## Strategic Relevance
Declining trust in institutions — especially media — is both a threat and an opportunity for RadMo. Threat: users may distrust *any* platform or curation system. Opportunity: the vacuum of trusted information sources creates demand for something new. The strategic reframe of RadMo as "you vs. companies selling you information for profit rather than truth" is validated by this data — people already believe this.

## Core Finding
The 2025 Edelman Trust Barometer (N=33,000+ across 28 countries) found that society has "devolved from fears to polarization to grievance" over the past decade. 61% of respondents globally report a moderate or high sense of grievance. Five of the 10 largest global economies are among the least-trusting nations. Media has been the least-trusted major institution since 2020. Nearly two-thirds of respondents find it difficult to differentiate reliable news from disinformation.

## Headline Stats
- **61%** globally report moderate-to-high institutional grievance
- **Media** is the least-trusted institution (has been since 2020)
- **~65%** find it hard to tell real news from disinformation
- **30-point trust gap** between high-grievance and low-grievance populations
- Major democracies (US, UK, Germany, France) among the least-trusting

## Caveats
- Edelman is a PR firm; the Trust Barometer is a well-regarded but commercially produced survey — treat as Tier 2, not academic Tier 1
- "Grievance" is a construct defined by Edelman's methodology, not a standardized academic measure
- Trust in employers remains high (75%) even as trust in institutions falls — people trust their immediate communities more than abstract systems

## RadMo Implications
- "Declining trust in media" is not just a problem — it's the stated user pain point RadMo is solving
- The reframe ("you vs. companies selling you information for profit") maps exactly onto what the data shows people already believe
- Transparency about RadMo's business model (subscription, not ad-driven) is a direct response to the trust gap
- The 65% who can't distinguish real news from disinformation is the addressable market for a trust-differentiated platform
