# Trend 07: News Audiences Are Fragmenting, Fatiguing, and Fleeing Legacy Media

## Strategic Relevance
The news consumption landscape is structurally shifting in ways that create both the problem RadMo is solving (fragmented, partisan, trust-eroding information ecosystems) and the market opportunity (a vacuum for a trusted, accessible alternative). Understanding where audiences are going — and why — is essential for product design and positioning.

## Core Findings
The 2025 Reuters Institute Digital News Report (N~100,000 across 48 countries) documents a broad structural shift:
- Traditional media (TV, print, news websites) continue to lose engagement
- Social media and video platforms are the primary beneficiaries
- **40% of audiences sometimes or often avoid news** — up from 29% in 2017, a joint record high
- Among 18-24 year olds, **44% say social media is their main news source**
- Overall trust in news has flatlined at **40% for three consecutive years**
- The top reason for avoiding news: it negatively affects mood (48% in Germany study)

## Key Structural Shift: The "Platform Reset"
Major platforms (Meta, X, TikTok) have algorithmically de-prioritized traditional news content, driving audiences toward "newsfluencers" — personality-led commentary that feels more relatable but is less accountable. This is creating an alternative media ecosystem that is faster, more partisan, less verifiable, and more emotionally resonant.

### Key Specific Stats (from Reuters 2025)
- 40% news avoidance globally (up from 29% in 2017)
- Trust in news: 40% globally (stable for 3 years but historically low)
- 44% of 18-24 year olds: social media is main news source
- Video-based consumption rose from 52% (2020) to 65% (2025)
- US social media news use significantly higher than most developed nations (similar to Global South)

## Caveats
- Reuters Institute data is from early 2025 (fieldwork Jan 2025) — the media landscape continues to evolve rapidly
- "Trust in news" is self-reported and may not reflect actual consumption behavior
- News avoidance is partly a healthy response to genuine information overload — not purely a problem to be solved

## RadMo Implications
- The 40% news avoidance figure represents the core audience RadMo must reach — people who want to be informed but are burned out on traditional formats
- The shift toward personality-led commentary is an opportunity: RadMo's "radically moderate" identity framework gives users a persona to adopt, not just a service to use
- Mobile-first, video-forward, and accessible design are table stakes given where audiences are going
- The trust vacuum at 40% globally is the market: people want trusted information but don't have a platform they trust
- The US audience's unusual social-media-as-news behavior (comparable to Global South) suggests a particularly fragmented and opportunity-rich domestic market
