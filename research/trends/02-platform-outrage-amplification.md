# Trend 02: Engagement Algorithms Systematically Amplify Outrage

## Strategic Relevance
This is the structural diagnosis that RadMo's entire thesis rests on. The problem isn't users being bad; it's that the incentive system rewards anger. The reframe from "left vs. right" to "you vs. companies selling you information for profit rather than truth" is supported directly by this literature. This is the strongest single argument for why a platform with a different reward structure is needed.

## Core Finding
Engagement-based ranking algorithms — the systems that determine what users see on Twitter/X, Facebook, TikTok, etc. — systematically select for emotionally charged, out-group hostile content because that content drives engagement metrics (likes, replies, shares, dwell time). Users are shown more anger-inducing content *than they would choose if given direct control*. The algorithm exploits the gap between revealed preferences (what makes you click) and stated preferences (what you actually want).

## Headline Statistic
A pre-registered controlled experiment (N=806) found that Twitter's engagement-based algorithm amplified anger by **0.47 standard deviations** relative to a reverse-chronological baseline — a large, statistically significant effect. Users exposed to the algorithm's selections reported feeling worse about their political out-group.

## Caveats
- Most experimental work uses Twitter/X; platform-specific findings may not fully generalize to TikTok, Instagram, etc.
- The study measures anger amplification, not long-term attitude change — short-term effects may or may not compound
- Some research suggests echo chambers are less algorithmically driven and more user-driven (people actively seek partisan content); the truth is likely both

## RadMo Implications
- The core product argument: a platform that *doesn't* optimize for engagement-as-outrage will produce a measurably different emotional experience
- Subscription model over advertising model is a values statement that maps directly to this research
- Users intuitively dislike outrage-optimized content even when they engage with it — there's a market for a platform that respects stated preferences over revealed ones
- The 0.47 SD figure is a compelling and citable data point for marketing/press
