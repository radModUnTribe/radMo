# Ground News

## Overview
Ground News is the closest existing player to Radical Moderate. It aggregates news stories and provides bias ratings to help users understand their media blind spots.

**Founded:** ~2018  
**Founders:** Harleen Kaur (CEO, former NASA engineer), Sukh Singh (CTO)  
**HQ:** Toronto, Canada  
**Team size:** ~18 employees  
**Funding:** ~$1.01M  
**Est. Annual Revenue:** ~$5.7M (strong capital efficiency — ~6x funding in ARR)  
**Business model:** Subscription  

## What They Do
- Aggregates news from hundreds of sources
- Assigns bias ratings (left / center / right) to sources and stories
- Shows users which stories their preferred sources are ignoring
- Operates as a news literacy tool — not a social platform

## Gaps Relative to Radical Moderate
- No social layer — users don't interact with each other
- News literacy framing vs. identity/community framing
- No gamification or reward structure for behavior change
- No cross-viewpoint conversation mechanics
- Does not address *why* people consume partisan media (incentives, identity) — only surfaces the fact that they do

## Strategic Positioning
Ground News owns the "news bias awareness" space. Radical Moderate's opportunity is the **news + social hybrid** space — which has no credible consumer player. Ground News is a tool; Radical Moderate is a community and identity.

## Notes
- Revenue figures are estimates from third-party sources and should be treated as directional, not precise
- Ground News is worth watching as a potential acqui-hire target or partner, not a head-on competitor
