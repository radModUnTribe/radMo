# RadMo Project Brief

**Project Name:** Radical Moderate (RadMo / radMod)

**Core Mission:** Rewire social media and news consumption incentives away from outrage and tribalism toward cross-viewpoint engagement and epistemic quality.

## Foundational Thesis

Incentives drive behavior, not moral appeals. Rather than asking users to be better people, RadMo creates pull through status, dopamine substitutes, and epistemic credibility—making moderation feel rebellious rather than weak.

## Identity & Positioning

**Target Identity:** "Radically moderate" — the quiet majority looking for an alternative to tribal polarization.

**Reframing:** From "left vs. right" to "you vs. institutions selling you information for profit rather than truth."

**Tone Model:** Tim Urban — accessible, humorous, non-elitist, pattern-recognizing.

## Why It Matters

- Most users don't perceive media bias as a problem requiring active solution
- Younger voters show pragmatic, issue-based behavior over tribal loyalty
- Community Notes proved bridging works; its limitation is the two-axis constraint
- RadMo's cross-cultural/multi-dimensional model is a genuine product evolution, not just a feature copy
- Transparency (explicit business model) is differentiation, not just ethics

## Long-Term Ambition

Influence governmental policy through cultural change by becoming the platform where epistemic quality and cross-viewpoint engagement create a measurably better information environment.
