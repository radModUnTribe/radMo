# RadMo Product Context

## User Problem & Pull

**Primary Tension:** Media consumption is fragmented across tribal silos; users want to understand opposing viewpoints but platforms actively discourage it through engagement mechanics (outrage loops, variable reward).

**Secondary Tension:** Algorithmic bias is invisible; users can't distinguish signal from noise without manual source triangulation.

**RadMo's Pull:**
- Status through credibility (not engagement metrics)
- Cooling period (24-72hr delay on reactions) makes the app less addictive—users choose quality over dopamine
- Cross-ideological engagement becomes the path of least resistance
- Transparency: explicit business model messaging builds trust vs. engagement-driven advertising

## Target Audience

**Primary:** "Concerned centrists" — pragmatic, issue-focused, uncomfortable with tribal framing

**Secondary:** Researchers, journalists, policy analysts seeking high-signal information

**Tertiary:** People fatigued by polarization media (Reddit users, Rogan/Carlson listeners seeking alternative)

## Competitive Landscape

### Ground News (Reference Case)
- Founder: Harleen Kaur (former NASA engineer) — capital-efficient operator
- Model: News aggregation + bias ratings (left/center/right binary)
- Strengths: Clean UX, transparent bias scoring, subscription-sustainable
- Ceiling: Binary left-right model doesn't capture cross-cultural nuance; secondary source aggregation

### RadMo's Differentiation
- Not aggregation (original content, cross-viewpoint submissions)
- Beyond binary: multi-dimensional credibility + behavioral proof (not just algorithmic labels)
- Prediction markets + reputation system to surface underreported topics
- Community Notes' bridging algorithm as foundation, evolved to cross-cultural framing

## Business Model

**Transparency as feature:**
- Subscription for advanced features (muted in early phases; emphasize credibility over paywall)
- No engagement-driven advertising
- Explicit communication: "We're not selling your attention"

## Key Insights

- Most users don't seek a "better information environment"—they're not in pain
- Dopamine gap problem: Why stay on RadMo if the cooling period removes the reward loop?
- "Why open RadMo" motivation must be stronger than X/Twitter/Reddit alternatives
- Reddit users routinely "bot-check" suspicious commenters—behavioral credibility is a proven user need

## Generational Tailwind

Younger voters (2020+) showing:
- Pragmatic, issue-based voting over tribal loyalty
- Comfort with nuance and conditional thinking
- Skepticism toward institutional narratives
- Validation for RadMo's positioning
